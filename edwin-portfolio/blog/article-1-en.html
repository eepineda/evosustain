From Arroyo Dulce to Europe

My story begins in Arroyo Dulce, a small community in the province of Barahona, in the Dominican Republic. I grew up in a humble environment deeply shaped by values such as hard work, discipline and a strong sense of community.

I completed my primary education in Arroyo Dulce and later continued my secondary studies in the municipality of Enriquillo. During those years I developed a growing interest in computing and technology, a field that was still emerging in our environment but already represented a path toward opportunity and progress.

After finishing high school, I started university studies at the Autonomous University of Santo Domingo (UASD), at its Barahona campus. During that period, I also gained my first experience related to data work and information systems, which helped me begin building an early professional foundation.

In 2007, while I was still at university, a family reunification process changed the direction of my life. I migrated to Spain at a young age, starting a new chapter defined by adaptation, learning and the challenge of building a future in a new country.

That transition not only transformed my personal context, but also opened the path to an international career in technology that would later continue across Spain and the United Kingdom.
