Education as a Driver of Transformation

After arriving in Spain, I had to begin a process of adaptation both personally and academically. One of the first steps was validating my studies from the Dominican Republic so that I could continue my education within the Spanish educational system.

During this period, I also had the opportunity to work as an IT instructor at the Centro Hispano-Dominicano, an organization focused on supporting the social and professional integration of the Dominican community in Spain.

My role involved teaching basic computing and digital literacy to people seeking to improve their employment opportunities and their ability to function in an increasingly digital environment. That experience reinforced an idea that still guides me today: sharing knowledge creates real impact in people’s lives.

Education not only transformed my professional path, but also shaped my understanding of technology as a tool for inclusion, social mobility and human development.

Over time, that same educational foundation enabled me to move into larger-scale projects within major organizations, without ever losing the conviction that learning and teaching are part of the same journey.
