From Public Administration to Digital Transformation

Over time, my career developed through technology projects within major organizations. One of the first important milestones was my involvement, through HP, in projects for the Spanish Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

In that environment, I worked on systems related to embassies and consulates, including pioneering initiatives connected to visa centralization and international information management. It was a key stage in understanding the importance of reliability, security and coordination in mission-critical systems.

I later moved into energy sector projects with IBM for CEPSA, where I contributed to initiatives related to data, infrastructure and digitalization. That experience further strengthened my profile within complex corporate environments.

Later, at Telefónica, I participated in technology infrastructure and critical migration projects for major organizations such as Repsol, Ferrovial, Talgo and Quirónsalud. These projects required not only technical expertise, but also strategic vision, coordination and the ability to execute in high-responsibility environments.

This professional evolution allowed me to consolidate a career focused on technology platforms, critical infrastructure and digital transformation within large-scale organizations.
